My Favorite Movies

  • Inception
  • The Dark Knight
  • Interstellar
  • The Grand Budapest Hotel

Inception is a combo of sci-fi, action, and thriller that is beautifully directed by Christopher Nolan. The movie's cinematography and special effects are jaw-dropping, and Inception was the first of its kind to push the boundaries of plot complexity and physical effects. To top it off, the star-studded cast act with incredible expression and deliberation, and the scores of Hans Zimmer level Inception to a whole new depth. It's truly a one-of-a-kind cinematic experience.

The plot of Inception revolves around the concept of implanting true inspiration into someone's mind. The main character, Dom Cobb, is able to enter people's dreams and specializes in stealing their secrets. In order to return home, Cobb and his team must perform inception on an executive, effectively convincing him to give up his corporation. Danger lies in performing inception, and if they fail, they risk their minds being lost in the dream world indefinitely -- they won't wake up.

The Dark Knight is a combo of thriller and action that is also directed by Christopher Nolan. The movie's dark themes and plotline are heavily nuanced and thematic, and bring Batman to a new level of darkness. Heath Ledger, the Joker, is the acting prodigy of this movie, perhaps inspired by dark thoughts of his own -- or vice versa. Hans Zimmer's masterpieces are featured, and darken the movie even further.

The plot of The Dark Knight revolves around the Batman attempting to end the atrocities committed in Gotham by the Joker, all the while battling with public perception of the Batman's role in justice and crime. Batman, aka Bruce Wayne, risks losing himself in the battle over Gotham, and also risks losing the ones he loves. Will the Batman or the Joker win, and at what cost?

Interstellar is a surprisingly scientifically accurate story of sci-fi, familial love, and suspense. This movie is also directed by Christopher Nolan and features Hans Zimmer's music (see a theme?). This movie makes each viewer question their perception of time, and causes the viewer to feel a wide range of emotion as the plot progresses. Simply put, seeing Matthew McConaughey cry will make you cry.

The plot of Interstellar revolves around a dystopian Earth, where food is a scarcity and the air is becoming unbreathable. The only option left is to leave Earth and find another planet, and Cooper steps up to this challenge -- at a price. His journey will take him far from his family, and for an indefinite period of time, ranging from a couple years to decades. But it's necessary to save his children and all the people of Earth. What will he find?

The Grand Budapest Hotel is a combo of comedy and mystery, draped with an atmosphere of whimsicality. The music score is equally whimsical, and the cinematography is both simple and unique. This is definitely one movie you will never forget, either because of its quirkiness, because of its endearing plotline, or both. The color themes and visuals will transport you to a whole other world.

The plot of The Grand Budapest Hotel revolves around the mysterious death of a wealthy old woman, who has left a most treasured art piece to the owner of the Grand Budapest Hotel, Gustave. With the help of his lobby boy, Zero, Gustave sets out to solve the mystery of the murder -- before her money-grabbing family gets to them first. The pair go on a fantastic journey and bond through their similarites and adventures.
